Zona Especial de Desarrollo Mariel

Cuba's Premier Economic Zone

The Zona Especial de Desarrollo Mariel (ZED Mariel) was established in 2013 under Decree-Law 313 as Cuba's first and only Special Economic Development Zone. Spanning 465 km² around the deep-water Port of Mariel, it is managed by the ZED Mariel Office and designed to attract foreign direct investment with a regulatory framework unmatched on the island. Critically, ZED Mariel allows 100% foreign ownership of enterprises — a landmark provision in Cuba's economic landscape.

  • 100% foreign ownership permitted — no joint venture required
  • Strategic location 45 km west of Havana with deep-water port access
  • Dedicated single-window approval process for streamlined setup
  • Modern infrastructure including roads, utilities, and telecommunications
Tax & Legal Framework

Investor Incentives

ZED Mariel offers a comprehensive incentive package designed to reduce costs, simplify operations, and maximize returns for international investors.

💰

Tax Holiday

10-year exemption from profit taxes upon approval. After the holiday period, a reduced rate of 12% applies — compared to the standard 35% corporate tax rate on mainland Cuba.

📋

Customs Benefits

Duty-free imports on equipment, raw materials, and construction supplies for zone operations. Streamlined customs procedures through a dedicated port clearance process.

🏢

Full Ownership

100% foreign ownership of enterprises is permitted within the zone. No local partner or joint venture structure is required — a first in Cuba's investment framework.

💱

Profit Repatriation

Full repatriation of profits and dividends is guaranteed by law. Investors retain complete control over earnings with no restrictions on capital transfers abroad.

Process

Investment Process

From initial inquiry to operational launch, the ZED Mariel Office guides investors through a clear, structured approval process.

Step 1
Initial Inquiry

Submit an expression of interest to the ZED Mariel Office outlining your sector, proposed investment, and operational objectives. The office provides preliminary guidance and documentation requirements.

Step 2
Feasibility Study

Prepare a comprehensive technical and economic feasibility assessment. This includes market analysis, financial projections, environmental impact considerations, and workforce planning.

Step 3
Application

Submit the formal application package to the ZED Mariel Office, including your detailed business plan, corporate documentation, feasibility study, and proposed investment timeline.

Step 4
Evaluation

The ZED Mariel Commission conducts a thorough review of all submitted materials. The formal evaluation process typically takes 60 days, during which additional information may be requested.

Step 5
Approval & Setup

Upon approval, receive your concession agreement and begin establishing operations within the zone. The ZED Mariel Office continues to provide support through permitting, construction, and launch phases.
