History

From Colonial Port to Modern Terminal

Over 260 years of strategic significance — from naval operations to the Caribbean's most ambitious port development.

1762
British Naval Operations
British forces utilize Mariel Bay during the Seven Years' War, recognizing its deep natural harbor and strategic position along Cuba's northern coast.
1980
The Mariel Boatlift
125,000 Cuban citizens depart from the port of Mariel for Florida in one of the largest mass emigrations in Western Hemisphere history.
2010
Mega-Port Announced
The Cuban government announces the development of a modern mega-port at Mariel with support from Brazil's BNDES development bank and PSA International.
2014
Modern Terminal Opens
PSA International inaugurates the new container terminal with 702m of berth, Post-Panamax cranes, and 15m draft — the most advanced port facility in the Caribbean.
2024
ZED Mariel Expansion
62 businesses approved within the Zona Especial de Desarrollo Mariel, with $3.5 billion in committed investment from international partners across multiple sectors.
Operational Advantages

Why Mariel Matters

Three decisive advantages that position Mariel as the gateway to Cuba's economic future.

⚡

Strategic Location

45km west of Havana, commanding the gateway to the Gulf of Mexico. Unmatched proximity to US markets — just 90 nautical miles from Key West — with direct shipping lanes to Latin America and Europe.

🚢

Deep-Water Capability

15-meter draft accommodates Neo-Panamax vessels with no tide restrictions. Year-round operations in a naturally sheltered bay, enabling the largest container ships transiting the expanded Panama Canal.

🔗

Multimodal Connectivity

Direct rail link to Cuba's national railway network and immediate access to the Autopista Nacional (A1 highway). Dedicated freight corridor connects the port to Havana and the interior.
